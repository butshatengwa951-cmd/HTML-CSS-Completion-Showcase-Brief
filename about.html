About Me

Growing like a leaf in the forest of possibility

My Journey

My name is Butsha Tengwa, and my journey in design began in the space between the city and the quiet. That’s where I learned to study patterns — how systems hold together, how rhythm creates momentum. Weekend hikes turned into a habit of noticing what actually works, and that turned into a way of designing.

Over time, I built a practice around that instinct. I pair disciplined thinking with creative execution. Every project I take on comes down to one belief: good design should feel inevitable, like the shortest path between two points. I work in cycles, too. Build, assess, refine, go again. No wasted motion.

Today I operate at the intersection of technology, storytelling, and product craft. The places that shaped me still show up in my work: layered, resilient, always aimed higher. I’m patient with the process. But I’m not here to play small.

Personal Interests

Beyond my professional pursuits, I find myself drawn to experiences that ground me and deepen my connection to the natural world. These passions inform my creative perspective and keep my work authentic:

  • Documentary photography focused on environmental storytelling
  • Sustainable living practices and zero-waste design principles
  • Reading in nature and writing in the margins of books
  • Trail running and mindful walking meditation in natural spaces
